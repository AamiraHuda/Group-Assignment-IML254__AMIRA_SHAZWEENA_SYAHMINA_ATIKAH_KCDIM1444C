Study Guide

Educational Hub

Healthy tips, structures, and wellness directions compiled by our nail experts.

Guide 1 of 4

Understanding Nail Anatomy

Healthy nails begin with understanding their basic structure. Each nail is made up of the nail plate, nail bed, matrix, cuticle, and lunula. The matrix is where new nail cells are produced, while the cuticle helps protect the nail from bacteria and infection. Learning about these parts can help you take better care of your nails and recognize signs of damage early.

Guide 2 of 4

The Truth About Cuticles

Cuticles are a natural protective barrier that seals the space between your skin and nail. Cutting them too often can increase the risk of infection and irritation. Instead, soften them with warm water, gently push them back, and apply cuticle oil regularly to keep them healthy, moisturized, and looking neat.

Guide 3 of 4

Gel vs. Acrylic: Choosing Right

Both gel and acrylic manicures have their own benefits. Gel nails offer a glossy, natural appearance with flexibility, making them ideal for everyday wear. Acrylic nails are stronger and better suited for adding length or creating intricate nail art. Consider your lifestyle, maintenance routine, and desired look when choosing the option that's right for you.

Guide 4 of 4

Maximizing Manicure Lifespan

A beautiful manicure can last longer with proper care. Moisturize your hands and cuticles daily, wear gloves while cleaning, and avoid using your nails to open or scratch objects. Regular touch-ups every 2–3 weeks will help maintain both the appearance of your manicure and the health of your natural nails. Professional secrets is you need to avoid hot water lifting, daily protective cuticle oils, and protective gloves for cleaning tasks.
